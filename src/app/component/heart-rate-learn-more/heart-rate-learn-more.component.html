Heart rate

Heart rate is the number of times the heart beats per minute (BPM). The resting heart rate of an adult is generally between 55 to 80 BPM. However, it varies with age, gender, and other factors. The heart rate increases during exercise. Athletes with better cardio respiratory fitness usually have a slower heart rate than the average adult.

Heart rate zones

We record the duration of 5 heart rate zones. Each of them is suitable for different types of exercise.

  • When the heart rate is at 50%-60% of its maximum capacity, this is suitable for dynamic warm-ups before running and for cool-downs after running.

  • When the heart rate is at 61%-70% of its maximum capacity, the body mainly supplies energy for exercise by burning fat, which can effectively reduce fat or control your body fat rate.

  • When the heart rate is at 71%-80% of its maximum capacity, carbohydrates are the body's main source of energy, which can effectively strengthen your cardiopulmonary functions.
